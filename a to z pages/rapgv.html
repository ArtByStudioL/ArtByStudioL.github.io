Val D'or

EST 1962  to 2010

This is Royal Albert tableware for traditional finesse - Val d'Or is translucent white bone china in the baroque Montrose shape with fluted edges, and that extra touch of gold trim on the edges and handles. In short, gilt edged luxury for the tabletop.

For centuries gold has been the most precious metal in the world; wars have been waged over it, people have risked everything to find it and communities have been built around it. This town saw its share of gilded dreams. In 1923, gold was found in the area and the town name literally means “Valley of Gold”. The luxurious item is still mined here today among other ores and minerals. In 1979, the area was declared a provincial historic site due do its major role in the history of Quebec mining. These days the town of 32,000 doesn't take all of its pride from minings. With many outdoor splendours to enjoy, travellers will find that the most precious feature of the valley lies above ground.

Cup Shape:Montrose, Avon

The Ugly House.
The original welsh name is "ty hyll"which literally means house ugly. During the 15th century the committee of nant Conway was reduced almost to a wilderness by bands of savage outlaws who ravaged the countryside far and wide. The whole area of snowdonia appears to have been wild and desolate and only coastal areas were more or less subdued.
According to legend, ty hyll was built in 1475 by two outlaw brothers wanting somewhere to house their bands of vagabonds and at the same time display their strength and prowess.
In olden times there was a means by which a man could obtain a dwelling and a small patch of freehold land. If smoke rose from the chimney of a rough house and constructed between sunset and sunrise without the knowledge of the land owner the builder could claim the land freehold. This was known as ty yn nos, (house of the night).
A secluded site would be chosen and all materials assembled and with the aid of friends and family work commenced. The chimney was built first of small stones, then the walls out of wattle and turf. At dawn additional land could be claimed by the new owner throwing axes to north, south, east and west.
It is believed that this is how the ugly house was constructed by the brothers.
