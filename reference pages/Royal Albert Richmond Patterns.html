Richmond English Bone China

Richmond China was under the Allied English Potteries group of China Companies,
and it had patterns under the Richmond name that dated back to the 1950s.
Many of the Richmond Patterns started out as Shellely Patterns,
then were later made under the Royal Albert Name using an
Un-Named Royal Albert Bone China Backstamp,
Only four Richmond patterns had a Unique pattern name:
"Blue Poppy" " Blue Rock " “Rose Time” and "Wild Anemone"
In 1972, the Pearson Group acquired Royal Doulton, and merged it with Allied English Potteries and
the Richmond China patterns were absorbed under the Royal Albert name or were discontinued.

Richmond Set # 3

"Rose Time"

This pattern was First Released by Shelley China in the 1940s as “Rose Spray” in came Henley shape, the Dainty Shape and the Ripon Shape well as a few different colors as cabinet sets.
Then it was later released by Richmond China as “Rose Time” in the late 1950s through the 1960s in a Smooth shape, Montrose shape, and a Gainsborough Shape
The in the Late 1960s it was Released by Royal Albert as an Un-Named pattern in the Montrose and Gainsborough Shape
Then in the 1970s it was released in the Symphony Series in six different Colors in the Gainsborough Shape
And as part of the Wayside Series in the Shelley Shape after it’s original form.
